World Historical Events

The Arab Spring In 2010

The Arab Spring, starting in 2010, was a wave of protests across the Middle East and North Africa, sparked by the self-immolation of Mohamed Bouazizi in Tunisia. Citizens demanded political reform and the end of authoritarian regimes. Major uprisings occurred in Tunisia, Egypt, Libya, Syria, and more. While some governments fell, others led to ongoing conflict and instability in the region.


It started in Tunisia when a street vendor, Mohamed Bouazizi, set himself on fire in protest against police corruption and ill-treatment, sparking nationwide protests that led to the ousting of President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali. This movement quickly spread to other countries, including Egypt, Libya, Syria, Yemen, and Bahrain, where citizens demanded political reform, social justice, and an end to authoritarian regimes.The Arab Spring significantly impacted the region's political landscape but also resulted in various challenges like instability, power vacuums, and humanitarian crises.


The Civil Rights Movement in the 1960s in the United States was a pivotal period of activism aimed at ending racial segregation and discrimination, primarily in the southern states. Led by figures like Martin Luther King Jr., the movement used nonviolent protests, sit-ins, and marches to challenge unjust laws and societal norms, leading to landmark achievements like the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965, which significantly improved rights for African Americans. Something similar to that is the ongoing indigenous rights movements. Ongoing movements focus on protecting the rights, lands, and cultures of Indigenous peoples around the world. In regions like the Amazon, Australia, and North America, Indigenous communities have fought against environmental destruction, exploitation of their land, and the erosion of their traditional ways of life. These protests call for the recognition of their sovereignty, cultural heritage, and their vital role in preserving the environment. Some protests that are also ongoing until now is the Palestinian protests. Palestinian protests are an enduring struggle for self-determination, justice, and the end of Israeli occupation in the West Bank and Gaza. Protests have been ongoing for decades, often sparked by evictions, settlement expansions, and restrictions on Palestinian freedoms. Key movements like the Great March of Return in 2018 saw mass demonstrations in Gaza demanding the right to return to ancestral lands and protesting the blockade and harsh living conditions. The conflict remains a central issue in global discussions on human rights and international law.
